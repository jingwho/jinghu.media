I write about AI and enterprise software for people who have to make decisions about them.

Most AI coverage is either hype or panic. This isn't that. I read the papers, talk to the operators, and write what's actually happening — at 2nd Order Thinkers on Substack and on YouTube.

me in 10 seconds — or ninety, if you're thorough

Eight years in chemistry research — papers, citations, and a PhD I came very close to finishing. Ten years in tech after that: cybersecurity at Transport for London, blockchain, operational technology at Just Eat Takeaway, and running my own AI voice startup in 2020 before anyone was calling it generative AI.

Went independent in August 2024 to write full-time. Based in London.

No hot takes, no reactive pieces. I only write when the evidence holds, the second-order effect matters, and I’d still stand behind it in two years.

working together — for the work that doesn't fit in a newsletter

I work with a small number of clients each year. Three things I do:

Speaking. Keynotes, panels, off-record sessions on AI strategy, the enterprise software stack, and where the hype-to-reality gap is actually costing people money. Tech conferences, industry events, internal leadership offsites — I’m available for the right event.

Commissioned research. Deep-dive articles or private memos on a specific thesis — market sizing, due diligence on a category, a stress-test of a strategy you've already committed to. Delivered as a written piece, published off-platform under your name or as an unbranded internal deliverable. Previous bylines include Forbes. No ghostwriting of the newsletter itself.

Advisory. Ongoing relationships with a small number of funds, operators, and leadership teams who want a second brain on AI bets — what to buy, what to build, what to ignore. Usually a monthly retainer with a call cadence and ad-hoc memos.

the fine print — because it matters

  • I don't do sponsored posts on the newsletter or the channel. No native ads, no "in partnership with," no placed mentions.
  • Paid work happens off-platform — keynotes, private reports, retainers. It's never dressed up as editorial.
  • I invest in public markets, tech included. If I hold a position in a company I write about, I disclose it at the top of that post — the position is usually too small to embarrass anyone but me, but the rule doesn’t have exceptions.
  • If something I write is wrong, I correct it — in the post, with a dated note, not quietly.
  • No AI-generated text pretending to be me. The writing is mine. The voice is mine. The mistakes are mine.
